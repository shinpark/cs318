Visit in the off season. Peak season in Venice means large crowds, expensive hotel rooms and restaurants hiking up their prices. For a great deal, plan on visiting Venice during the months of September and October for mild weather and lower prices. Carnival – which falls between March and April each year – is a great period to visit, but may bring higher hotel prices. Winter months are significantly cheaper than summer, but the risk of acqua alta (high water) flooding may not be for everyone (read more about Acqua Alta here).

Choose a family run B and B or rent a short-term apartment. Skip the large hotels and opt for a family owned bed and breakfast for a cozy and budget friendly stay. We recommend 3749 Ponte Chiodo or renting your own apartment through rental sites like AirBnb. Need help deciding which neighborhood to stay in? Check out our guide of the city’s sestieri.
